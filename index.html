Research Focus

My doctoral work at the IITB-Monash Research Academy explores the early colonization dynamics of Marinobacter adhaerens on weathered polymers. By mapping surface chemistry evolution through advanced metrology—including X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) and Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)—I evaluate how environmental weathering dictates the thermodynamic viability of microbial biotransformation for industrial application.

I bridge the gap between lab-scale surface chemistry, synthetic biology, and regulatory frameworks. I am deeply passionate about learning from the agility of deep-tech startups and venture capital—understanding how to rapidly iterate, de-risk scientific hypotheses, and build scalable ventures that turn environmental science into industrial-scale biomanufacturing.
